Maneka Gandhi vs. Union of India

January 25 1978

Maneka Gandhi vs. Union of India, AIR 1978 SC 597, was a landmark decision of the Supreme Court of India in which the Court significantly expanded the interpretation of Article 21 of the Constitution of India.

The decision had a significant influence on Indian constitutional law and has been described as the moment when the Supreme Court of India rejected "three decades of formalist interpretation, and inaugurated a new path where Courts would expand the rights of individuals against the State, instead of limiting or contracting them.

The major headline of the Maneka Gandhi v. Union of India case is that the Supreme Court of India expanded the interpretation of Article 21 of the Constitution of India. The case was a landmark decision that established a relationship between Articles 14, 19, and 21 of the Constitution, which is known as the "golden triangle" or "trinity".

The case involved journalist Maneka Gandhi, whose passport was impounded by the government in 1977. Gandhi filed a petition challenging the impounding of her passport and the government's refusal to provide reasons, which led to the landmark ruling
